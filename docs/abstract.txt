SMARTFOREST: FOREST FIRE RISK PREDICTION USING ENVIRONMENTAL DATA
SRM Institute of Science and Technology | AIML-B | Mini Project 2026
SDG Goal 15: Life on Land

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
ABSTRACT
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Forest fires are among the most devastating natural disasters affecting global
ecosystems, causing irreversible damage to biodiversity, carbon storage, air
quality, and human settlements. Early detection and risk prediction remain
critical challenges in environmental management and disaster preparedness.

This project, SmartForest, presents a machine learning-based approach to
predict forest fire risk zones using environmental and meteorological parameters.
The Algerian Forest Fires Dataset from the UCI Machine Learning Repository,
consisting of 244 observations from two regions (Bejaia and Sidi Bel-abbes)
collected over the summer of 2012, serves as the primary data source.

Key environmental features analyzed include temperature, relative humidity,
wind speed, rainfall, and fire weather indices — FFMC (Fine Fuel Moisture
Code), DMC (Duff Moisture Code), DC (Drought Code), ISI (Initial Spread
Index), BUI (Buildup Index), and FWI (Fire Weather Index).

The project follows a complete data science pipeline: data collection,
preprocessing, exploratory data analysis, visualization, and predictive
modeling. Three classification algorithms are implemented and compared:
Logistic Regression, Decision Tree, and Random Forest. The Random Forest
model achieves the highest accuracy (~97%), with FWI and Temperature
identified as the strongest predictors.

Results demonstrate that machine learning can effectively support forest
fire risk assessment, contributing to proactive environmental management
and aligning with the UN Sustainable Development Goal 15: Life on Land.
