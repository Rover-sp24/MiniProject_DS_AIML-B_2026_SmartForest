PROBLEM STATEMENT

TITLE:
Forest Fire Risk Prediction Using Environmental Data

DOMAIN:
Environmental Analytics / Machine Learning

SDG ALIGNMENT:
UN Sustainable Development Goal 15 – Life on Land

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
BACKGROUND
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Forest fires are one of the most severe natural hazards worldwide, posing
serious threats to ecological systems, human health, and socioeconomic
infrastructure. With climate change intensifying heat and drought conditions,
the frequency and severity of forest fires are increasing globally.

Traditional fire monitoring systems are largely reactive — relying on satellite
imagery, manual patrol, or post-ignition detection — leading to delayed response
and greater damage. There is a critical need for proactive, data-driven tools
that can identify high-risk conditions before a fire occurs.

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
PROBLEM DEFINITION
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Given historical environmental data including temperature, humidity, wind
speed, rainfall, and fire weather indices from forest monitoring stations,
build a machine learning classification model that can:

  1. Predict whether a given set of environmental conditions will result
     in a forest fire (binary classification: fire / not fire).

  2. Identify the most influential environmental factors contributing
     to fire risk.

  3. Provide visual insights into seasonal and regional fire patterns.

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
RESEARCH QUESTIONS
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  Q1. Which environmental parameters are most correlated with fire occurrence?
  Q2. Which machine learning model achieves the best classification accuracy?
  Q3. Are there seasonal or regional patterns in fire occurrence?
  Q4. Can FWI (Fire Weather Index) alone predict fire risk reliably?

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
EXPECTED OUTCOMES
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  - A trained classification model with >90% accuracy
  - Feature importance analysis identifying key fire predictors
  - Visual dashboards of EDA and model performance
  - Documented pipeline reproducible via GitHub
